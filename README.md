# Blinkit Grocery Sales Dashboard — Power BI

## Overview
End-to-end Power BI analysis of Blinkit's sales performance, customer satisfaction, and inventory distribution across outlet types, sizes, and locations.
---
## Business Objective
Blinkit needed clarity on what drives revenue — product type, fat content, outlet size, geography, or establishment age. This dashboard answers those questions with interactive visuals and KPI tracking built for operational decision-making.
---
## KPIs Tracked
| Metric | Description |
| Total Sales | Overall revenue across all items and outlets |
| Average Sales | Mean revenue per transaction |
Number of Items | Distinct item count sold |
| Average Rating | Customer satisfaction score per item |
---
## Dashboard Breakdown
**Total Sales by Fat Content** — Donut chart showing revenue split between Regular and Low Fat products, with secondary KPI overlays.
**Total Sales by Item Type** — Horizontal bar chart ranking all product categories by revenue. Exposes top and bottom performers instantly.
**Fat Content by Outlet** — Stacked column chart comparing product preference across individual outlets — useful for localized assortment decisions.
**Sales by Outlet Establishment Year** — Line chart correlating outlet age with revenue maturity.
**Sales by Outlet Size** — Pie chart showing revenue share across Small, Medium, and Large formats.
**Sales by Location Tier** — Funnel chart mapping Tier 1 / 2 / 3 city performance.
**All Metrics by Outlet Type** — Matrix table giving a full KPI snapshot across Grocery Stores and Supermarket Types 1–3.
---
## Key Insights
- Medium-sized outlets generate the highest share of total revenue
- Supermarket Type 1 leads every KPI across the board
- Tier 3 cities show strong sales — underleveraged growth market
- Regular fat products dominate sales; Low Fat is a niche opportunity
- Outlets established 2015–2018 represent peak revenue maturity
